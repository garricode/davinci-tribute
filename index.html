Leonardo di ser Piero da Vinci

One of the greatest painters of the High Renaissance.

Timeline

1452 - Leonardo da Vinci is born on April 15, in Vinci.
1457 - Leonardo moves to his father's home in Vinci at the age of five.
1460 - Leonardo moves to Florence with his father.
1467 - Leonardo is apprenticed to Andrea del Verrocchi, in Florence.
1472 - Leonardo becomes a member of the painter's guild in Florence.
1473 - Leonardo draws a landscape on the Feast of Santa Maria delle Neve.
1476 - Leonardo is charged with sodomy on April 9, but is released from the charges on June 16.
1477 - Leonardo paints a part of the Annunciation to the Virgin.
1478 - Leonardo records in his notebooks that he has begun working on "two Virgin Marys." He also paints the Portrait of Ginevra de Benci.
1481 - Leonardo is comissioned to paint the Adoration of the Magi.
1482 - Leonardo moves from Florence to Milan, and enters the patronage of Ludovico Sforza.
1483 - Leonardo recieves a comission to paint the Virgin of the Rocks.
1485 - Leonardo paints Lady with an Ermine.
1489 - Leonardo studies anatomy.
1490 - Leonardo begins to work on a bronze horse for his patron Ludovico Sforza, as well as a book on landscape and hydraulic works, but never finishes.
1493 - Leonardo's mother becomes a member of his household.
1495 - Leonardo begins painting the Last Supper in the convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie.
1496 - Leonardo illustrates mathematician Fra Luca Pacioli's De devina proportione.
1498 - Leonardo decorates the walls and ceiling of the Sala delle Asse. He also attempts planning a flying machine.
1499 - Leonardo leaves Milan after the French invasion.
1500 - Leonardo and Pacioli go to Mantua, then Leonardo continues alone to Florence. In Mantua, he draws the Portrait in Profile of Isabelle d'Este. In Florence, he paints the Virgin and Child with Saint Anne.
1502 - Leonardo becomes Cesare Borgia's military, and travels around the Romagna inspecting fortifications.
1503 - In Florence again, Leonardo begins the Battle of Anghiari. Also, he trys to divert the Arno River away from Pisa in order to defeat Pisa in war.
1504 - Leonardo's father dies on July 9.
1505 - Leonardo attempts a second time to build a flying machine, begins sketches for the Mona Lisa, and gives up painting the Battle of Anghiari.
1506 - In May, Leonardo is summoned to Milan by Charles d'Amboise, the French governor.
1507 - Leonardo is appointed Louis XII's painter and engineer. He paints a second version of the Virgin on the Rocks, meets his beautiful assistant Francesco Melzi, and travels yet again to Florance pursuing a lawsuit against his brothers concerning his inheritance from his deceased uncle.
1508 - Leonardo returns to Milan and spends a long period studying anatomy, town-planning, optics, and hydraulic engineering.
1513 - Leonardo moves to Rome, and while living in the Vatican studies the properties of mirrors.
1515 - Leonardo paints John the Baptist and constructs a mechanical lion for the coronation of the new king of France, Francis I.
1516 - Leonardo permanently leaves Italy for France, where he serves Francis I at court in Amboise.
1519 - Leonardo dies on May 2 at Cloux France, and is buried in the Church of St. Valentine at Amboise.

Studies

The geology of the earth, with its mountains, valleys, rivers and rocks.
The anatomy of the human body with its skeleton, muscles, veins and internal organs. Leonardo was given dead bodies by a hospital. He "dissected" (cut up) thirty dead bodies and carefully drew many of the parts. His drawings of bones and muscles were to help other artists to paint the human body properly.
The anatomy of horses, cows, dogs, and bears.
The expressions on human faces.
The flight of birds.
The weather and its phenomena.
The way that water flows.
The botany of plants.
Light, shadows, mirrors and lenses.
Perspective and the way to make things look near or far.
The geometry of solid objects. He drew many careful pictures which were used by the mathematician Luca Pacioli in a book called De Divina Proportione.
